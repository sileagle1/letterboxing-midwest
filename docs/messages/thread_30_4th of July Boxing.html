From: Roze (rozebud@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2006-07-06 11:10:22 UTC-07:00
> > Anyone spend time boxing over the holiday?

We went out twice, looking for four boxes and found three. First, we
hit Babler Park in Chesterfield to find the "Snake Doctor" box planted
there this past May. Although we were pretty sure we were in the
correct spot, we did not find the box, so I used the "contact the
placer" link to inquire. Then we drive south a few miles to the Rock
Wood Reservation - this was a very cool place, as the last remaining
lime kiln in the St. Louis area is there. A short walk and a bit of
poking around in the rocky ledge area earned us the "Rock Woods" box -
planted back in 2000! It was still in fine shape, so if you never got
this one, it's still waiting for you!

Next day, we went back to Queeny Park to get the last two boxes in the
"Queens" series. If you are in the St. Louis area, i HIGHLY recommend
you look for these - they are BEAUTIFUL hand-carved stamps, created by
the Fairy Princess Fiona. Clues for seven of them are on-line; once
you find them and work the little puzzle, FPF will send you the link
for the final box.

Sadly, the first time we went we found one box destroyed, the log and
stamp missing. She replaced that one in a new and hopefully more
secure spot. She told me that a second one turned up missing, and
she's now ready to re-plant it - we're going to meet up tomorrow, so I
can still be the first person to stamp in on the log. I'm so happy
that my new hobby of letterboxing has now led me to make a new friend
in the area!

Hope everyone had a happy and safe 4th!

rozebud

Re: 4th of July Boxing

From: ks_letterboxers (ks_letterboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-09 02:10:10 UTC
We had a big day of boxing here in hot KC today. We visited Craw &
Joey's new boxes at Liberty Memorial and also got three boxes from
their fountains of KC series.

If you're in the KC area either now, but especially in December and
thereafter, be sure and check out the expanded museum complex at
Liberty memorial. For out-of-towners, the memorial is the only museum
to WWI veterans in the nation. We talked to one of the volunteers
there and she said the new museum will include lots of interactivity
for kids plus rotating exhibits in the current museum space. (Plus,
don't forget those great boxes you can pick up!)

Allison
Wyld Blueberries
