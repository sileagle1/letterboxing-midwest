We are the Shakespeare Festival of St. Louis. Each year since 2001, we
have constructed a set from the ground up in Forest Park, just east of
Art Hill (between the art museum and the zoo). We cast national and
local talent, both Equity and non. Then, weather permitting, we have
23 nights (every night at 8PM except Tuesdays) of free Shakespeare for
anyone who cares to come. I believe our one-night attendence record is
around 5,400 people! This was a Thursday night, after several days of
rain when attendance dropped to just a few hundred folks, and the
forecast for the upcoming (final) weekend was not looking too
promising...

I always recommend folks plan to come early in the run rather than wait
till the end, just in case the weather turns. Last year, we only had
one rain-out, plus a couple of delayed starts. At that time of year,
we tend to get a lot of late-afternoon thunderstorms, which makes for a
fun day in the office:

Phone: "Ring-ring."
Me: "Shakespeare Festival of St. Louis."
Phone: "Hi, are you planning to have a show tonight? I mean, with the
rain and all."
Me: "We make every attempt to perform each night. In case of inclement
weather, we could delay the start of the show up to one hour. As long
as we can keep the stage dry and conditions safe for the performers and
crew, we will have a show. Please plan to bring chairs, or at least a
tarp to put your blanket on, as the ground will be wet."

I get to make this speech many many many times each day the weather
threatens...I spend a lot of time looking at the hourly forecast on
weather.com!

If you are interested, here is the website: www.sfstl.com. It's not a
very good website, but we're working on it!

I've got a very nice drawing of Shakespeare that I'm planning to use
for one stamp, but kind of want to figure out some images that relate
more to this specific play, which is basically just two convoluted love
stories. I really need to sit down and read it thru and see what
strikes my fancy, which (now that we have the director's cut of the
script) I plan to start on during the holidays!

~ rozebud
