Hello from Spirit Lake, Iowa! Im new to letterboxing and would really like to get some action going here in Norhtwest Iowa. I have only found a few boxes and have placed one of my own and have adopted two local ones that havent been active for years. For right now they have store bought stamps but I am in the process of changing that. I bought some carving supplies and in the last few days carved a couple stamps. Its not that great either but im pretty impressed with my first attempts and im happy enough with it to put in a box. Yeah!! I will get the others replaced soon.

There isnt alot of boxes around this part of Iowa but at least now if you are passing through I have a couple for you to find.

I noticed how quiet it is. But sometimes it only takes one person to get things going. Haha! I was invited to join a group that is Letterboxing Great Lakes, its more Minnesota and Eastward, it is a very busy group. I also joined the basement one and cleared up at least one box for them in Hospers. We do alot of hiking and exploring in Minnesota but I do live in Iowa and will do most of my own boxes here. Thats good enough for me. :) I'm still in the paperwork stages for a (adopted) box in Spirit Lake, one for Kilen Woods State Park in Minnesota, and one at a nature center an hour from here. I also saw someone placed a box in Sanborn, Ia the other day and plan on finding that one next weekend. I havent found that many yet though. The two I have out there I recently replaced my store bought stamps with hand carved ones, one hasnt been found at all yet and the other had been found once by three people so I took that journal home to save. Haha! I was so excited. I enjoy the carving so I hope to get better at that. I would like to do a series sometime. Those are so cool to me. Havent found any yet though. I didnt do alot of traveling this summer. :( I have been spending alot of time on Atlas Quest, there is so much to learn and know about. Im pretty much still at the beginning stages. I cant wait to not be so much a newbie. Well enough rambling. If there is anyone out there closer to me feel free to email or post, would love to hear from you. Thanks for replying rozebud.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-08-14 23:25:41 UTC-05:00

Glad to hear you joined the Great Lakes group!  I didn’t initially suggest it since I saw you said you were in WESTERN Iowa.  We have attended two events in Iowa (one in Quad Cities area and one in Decorah) and have met several Iowa boxers at those.  We tend to include them in our Great Lakes thinking since there isn’t much else going on in a lot of the area in the official Midwest region.  That’s probably why Minnesota asked to be added to the Great Lakes region instead of Midwest – there was more activity.  MO has been busy, but it seems they tend to have more of a “state” focus rather than regional (they had a separate Mid-MO blog which used to be very active, but it seems to have gotten quieter.  Maybe they all move to AQ, which has happened in a few other areas of the country).

 

Anyway, just wanted to let you know that there ARE people who do read this forum, but many of us don’t actually live in the region!

 

Wisconsin Hiker

From: rozebud.rm (rozebud@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2009-08-15 12:59:52 UTC
A quick peek on AtlasQuest shows only 2 boxes within 25 miles of Spirit Lake, IA, so I'm guessing there's not a lot of active letterboxers in your area...hang in there! There wasn't much here in St. Louis when I started, but as they say, "plant, and they will come." We're starting to get quite a nice little community here, finally, after a few years of my mostly boxing on vacation (lucky for me, my home state of Delaware has a lot of activity, and I visit there fairly frequently...hmmm...it's been a while tho...time to plan a trip?).

So carve, plant, network online, take trips when you can! you're a long way from St. Louis, but if you ever make it down this way, let me know!
