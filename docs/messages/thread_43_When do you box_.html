Having boxed for less than a year (We started last October), We didn't
even think about how the vegetation would make finding a box planted in
a fairly easy place to find in April would be real hard to find in
June. It will definitely think about it next spring.

As far as a favorite time to box, I think spring and fall are our
favorite. We like a little green on the trees, but with so many hot
weekends this summer in KC, not to mention a nice case of poison ivy
and a couple of ticks (luckily not attached), I don't think June
through September is for us.

From: ks_letterboxers (ks_letterboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-18 16:24:21 UTC
Earlier in the week we hit some boxes on an Overland Park (KS) bike
trail. It's a series of 12 placed in December and this is the third
time for us to try and finish it all up. The first couple of times we
got some was in the spring. All the landmarks easily showed up, no
poison ivy, no chiggars, beautiful weather. In August, though, it's a
different landscape. We had a hard time telling where the last box
that we had found was located; one box we just said forget it as the
amount of poison ivy between the box and the bike path was an itch
gulf.

It's all just one more reason to like boxing -- whoever knew we had so
much vegetation in Kansas?

Hello! Sorry it has taken me a few days to respond to your e-mail. It was
great to meet your and your family. I'm glad you enjoyed the cave
experience so much.

Unfortunatly I'm busy the holiday weekend. however, Rock Bridge SP is a
fabulous place to visit, even without entering the Devils Ice Box. In
particular is Connor's Cave, which is open all year and is easy, and
interesting at the same time. If you'd like more info I'd be glad to help
you set up a day of exploring. The park also has a great Orienteering
course, with maps available at the park office.

Just a thought, but have you ever heard of a hobby called Letterboxing?
It's kind of like a treasure hunt where you print clues from the internet
then go about looking for hidden boxes which contain a logbook and a hand
carved stamp. I usually don't publicize this semi-secret hobby of mine, but
your family seems like a swell bunch. I do a lot of Letterboxing with my 5
year old son and he loves it. Might be something your family would enjoy.
Typicaly the clues are straight forward and only require a compass. While
it is similar to geo-caching, I believe it is far superior. No need for
hi-tech gear. LB is much more 'under the radar' than geo-caching and we
kind of like it that way. If you do go, PLEAS re-hide any box you find
completely. Some folks leave them exposed and the box goes missing. If
you're interested take a look at www.letterboxing.org and you can also see
some of the things our local LB (Letterboxing) group has done at, well, the
address escapes me, but run a search for Mid Missouri Letterboxers. You
should quickly find our blog. Browse through the archives and you'll see
some of our gatherings. Wow! What a ramble.

Hopefuly we can catch up sometime and crawl thorugh a cave or two.

Keep in touch!

Ron
