What if we scrapped the states completely and went for a more
symbolic idea?

I was thinking about this while at McDonalds sucking down a big
breakfast this morning, so pardon any glitches in logic.


Say we went with a round patch. The outer area rimmed in black or
some dark framing color.

Did one of those 'amber waves of grain' background colors. Or a
large close up of a wheat and corn in the center part. Superimpose
Letterboxing Midwest in the center.
Around the perimeter inside the frame would be the 2 letter state
designations in alphabetical order. Or, to eliminate having some of
the states be read upside down, then layer them in 2 arcs, one at the
top, consisting of 4 states, the one at the bottom (for visual weight
reasons) having 5 states? i.e.:


=========================
AE * IA * KS* KY

Letterboxing
Midwest

MO * NE * ND * OK * SD
=========================



the above would have a background picture either a swath of color
layers or the corn and the wheat?

MoWizLiz
I have the ideas, just not the know-how to do this suggestion.
(laughing)

From: Silent Doug (silentdoug@letterboxing.info) | Date: 2007-06-29 21:29:17 UTC-04:00
At 11:50 AM 6/18/2007, you wrote:

>What if we scrapped the states completely and went for a more
>symbolic idea?

The Pacific Northwest patch that Funhog designed is a banana slug
holding an umbrella, and the patch is shaped like a raindrop -- so it
represents unique and common things about the region.

I think this is a great approach for the midwest -- just think of
things that represent the region, and I bet a patch idea will come
into focus pretty quickly.

The silo is great, I think. What animals do you think of in the
region? What about the topography? I think of the plains and the
prairies, and then prairie dogs, and bison.

What other icons of midwest America can you think of?
