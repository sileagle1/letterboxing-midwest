From: Cyndie (CyndieLLong@aol.com) | Date: 2006-06-08 23:00:59 UTC
Hi all. My name is Lock Wench and I am planning a planting (and
finding!) event for North Dakota in May 2007. After noticing that
North Dakota had fewer letterboxes than any other state, some
friends and I started talking about remedying that. I put out some
feelers on the bigger lists and was shocked and amazed at the
response. It ran about 99% positive, with lots of people offering to
send boxes if they couldn't go there themselves, some people signing
up right away even though it is a year from now! and 1% concerned
that the boxes would languish and not be maintained.

People from the west and east coast who have boxed with me know what
a freak I am about doing things 150%, so they chuckled at that last
one. When I am challenged, it makes me even more determined! On
that note, I want you to know that I have been in contact with
representatives from the Chamber of Commerce of Fargo, North Dakota,
and am working to set up a two day event to include free instruction
for newboxers, a planting frenzy..and a big search to wrap up the
weekend.

I would really appreciate it if any North Dakota boxers could
contact me to help me coordinate the little details. Come spread a
little letterboxing love! Feel free to forward this post. Thanks!

Lock Wench

Lock Wench,

We did something similar to this last year in Texas.
There were three counties adjacent to one another
without a single box in any of them. We had a
gathering with the purpose of focusing on planting in
those three counties. Some folks couldn't come for
the gathering, but they sent boxes ahead for others to
plant. Some folks couldn't come that weekend, so they
planted at another time. That area now has 23 boxes,
less than one year after the event. We all agreed
that we would all do maintenance as we could and as we
were near or passing through that area.

We styled it as a "barnraising" - a community event in
the American frontier as settlers moved to a new area
and had to get set to go quickly. Because none of us
were from that area, I did tons of research and posted
it to webpages on the event as I found interesting
things so folks could refer to my website for ideas
for boxes. Of course, because letterboxers tend to be
very compulsive, most of the attendees also did their
own research!

The one thing I am disappointed about from the
gathering is that we haven't developed more boxers in
that area. We still only have a small handful that
live in those counties. However, we did nothing to
encourage new boxers, thinking that the boxes would be
in place for the eventual moment when they show up.

I realize publicity and so forth is very
controversial, but one simple local article made a
huge difference in my own area. We went in my county
from 3 boxes at the end of 2003 to 47 today. A good
reporter can surely make a big difference!

If you are interested in taking a look at our event,
the pages are still posted at:

www.geocities.com/barefootlucy/JENBarnraising

You are planning a much more complex affair, but maybe
you can get an idea or two!

Also, I would love to send the event box from our
barnraising to your planting event next year, so if
you are interested in having it, put it on your list
and let me know when you want me to send it along.

Good luck and have fun putting it together!

Lucy
