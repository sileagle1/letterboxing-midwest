From: mowizliz (Liz@morewisdom.com) | Date: 2007-04-03 19:21:33 UTC
Hi everyone,

First...welcome to all the new people who have recently signed up to be
part of our reserved (read that as quiet) MidWest group!

After reading over at the Great Lakes and Newboxers groups about
people's first finds, I have a question for y'all. What was your first
box that you planted? Is it still there? Would you change the
location? What made you plant your first box in that location? Did
you "mama hen" it for a while? You know, checking in on it frequently?

My very first box, a Do Tell! thing made remained at its location for
roughly 8 or so months before going missing. Whoever even took the
rock that was sitting over it. In all fairness, we had a rash of boxes
go missing for a while here in Louisville over a year or two. When one
would go mia, so would several others, so I'm guessing it was someone
hitting them all at one time. Remarkably, I've got one about 6 feet
from that spot that has been there for over a year and a half. I chose
that park because it had a nice walking path, wasn't used very much,
and was in nice shape...plus very close to the house.

I know that over the past four or so years, my methods of planting and
what I choose to plant have changed tremendously. Learn and do, and
constantly adapt to beliefs and such. :-)

MoWizLiz
Louisville, KY

From: Atom 118 (hersh118@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-04-04 09:48:50 UTC-05:00
The first box I planted was "CP1" (which was just over a year ago). I
chose the location because: (A) I found a good out of the way place to
hide it (B) it is located near-by a bunch of 'odd' foot-traffic so any
potential finders would fit right in (C) I had a stamp that I had carved
more-or-less on a lark that would fit the location [and I was proud of
it] (D) I could post it without an exact address but one that could be
easily deciphered after a quick Google Search and (E) it would bring
people to an interesting part of town that is more commonly talked about
than visited. The location also allowed for what I though were
semi-tricky clues....it was my first box, I didn't want to make it super
easy!

I had planted it near my work so I could keep a close watch on it. When
the first few people would come and find it, I'd quickly go back to
check on it and see the stamps and any comments that were written. Now,
I hardly go look for it...just enough to keep myself convinced that it
is still there.

Atom 118

(from Chicago)

From: perdu_watcher (perdu_watcher@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-08 15:18:51 UTC
My first box was Mardi Gras-07. It was (still is) way too big, but
I wanted a feathered mask to cover it...well, I thought it would be
cool at the time. It still looks pretty cool, but way, way too
big. I included a First Finder Certificate that I designed for a
Mardi Gras and a Mardi Gras...umm...guardian.

I don't think I'd change the location.

The finders who've e-mailed me have so far enjoyed it. The only
thing this one has that no other of my plants have (other than the
ridiculously large box) is a store bought log. I've thought of
changing it out and maybe I will someday.
