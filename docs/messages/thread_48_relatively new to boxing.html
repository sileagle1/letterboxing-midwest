From: teqtn377 (Patience3963@netzero.com) | Date: 2006-09-21 20:08:51 UTC
Hi everyone! Just wanted to drop in and say hello to our fellow
boxers. I was reading an article in a magazine last month and
thought that it would be a good idea to pass on to some of my former
Girl Scout Leaders. You see, my oldest child is going into middle
school and she decided that she was getting too old for Girl Scouts
and wanted to try something else, anyway, I was talking to my
husband about the article and mentioned that maybe I should call the
council office. My children, ages 6, 9 and 12 overheard the
conversation and asked if this was something that we could try as a
new "mommy and me" project. I figured why not give it a try. What
else can you do that educates the kids, gets them out of the house
and working as a team, gets exercise for mom, and doesn't hardly
cost a thing? So the very next day we went on our first find. Let me
just say, we're hooked! The kids absolutely love figuring out the
clues and learning some history about our hometown. We're from St.
Joseph, MO. We just started about a month ago and we have already
found 14 boxes. Now of course the kids are ready to plant their own
box. The kids have decided to turn our "logbook" into a scrapbook,
so we just stamp on plain white paper and we use the timer on our
camera to take group pictures in cool spots around the trails, not
giving away the plant location of course! I want to thank those of
you who take the time and put out the effort to plant the boxes. It
is becoming a wonderful experience for my children as well as myself
and we tell everyone we know about our cool new activity! Happy
boxing!
Stacie
