Hi,

I'll preface this by saying that I'm not a huge one for asking
permission to plant on public land. If the letterbox is hidden well,
unobtrusive and well taken care of, I'm not too worried about whether
there is a "rule" against planting in that area. I haven't planted
(and probably wouldn't) in a national park because that seems to be
taboo. That being said, if there is a place I would ask permission
for, it would probably be military land, especially the way things
stand today. Anyone seen "hiding" anything on a military base might
end up in Guantanamo or a "secret" CIA prison ;). Let's make sure
that letterboxers are not labeled as enemy combatants, we do have
weapons of mass destruction (OK linoleum cutters and exacto knives),
and our carving materials could be miscontrued as plastic explosive.
You wouldn't want to see a letterbox destroyed by remote device.

All kidding aside, I think in this situation, I would definitely ask
permission (maybe go to the public relations officer/dept. first)
before planting a letterbox on a military base. I wouldn't hold my
breath about it, but you never know.

Craw (of Craw and Joey)

Hi, everyone - I normally just lurk on this list, but felt I'd chime
in on this one.

I work at a VA Medical Center (Hines, IL), which is actually it's own
"city" (as opposed to being located within a municipality's
jurisdiction). The Hines Police are federal officers, and they were
guarding our entrances 24/7 until about a year ago (now they just
guard evenings/nights/weekends). In these post-9/11 days, I think the
government would definitely frown upon a letterbox hidden on
government property, particularly a military base.
