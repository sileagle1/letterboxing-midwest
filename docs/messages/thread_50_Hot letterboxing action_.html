Hot letterboxing action!

From: rozebud.rm (rozebud@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-10 20:32:05 UTC
Greetings friends! I just got back from a frantic week of
letterboxing with my Mid-Atlantic friends, at the DE Games at
Killen's Pond State Park. Wow - what an event! Sewsowbizzy planted
over 70 boxes, and participants were given only one week to find as
many as they could - it was a blast!

Now I'm back home in St. Louis, and wondering, what next? There are
SO few boxes around my area. Yes, I'm going to plant some more over
the next few months, but seriously - how does one generate
interest? Any ideas?

I'm hoping to make my way mid-state in the next month or so; there
are loads of boxes to be had in Columbia and Fulton. But I'm about
tapped out on finds within an hour of home...I'm not jonesing yet,
but you know how it is when the letterboxing itch needs scratching!

Also, another question - if I were to host an event in the Lou',
would y'all attend? What would you be interested in seeing at said
event?

rozebud

From: Ron D (rmed_mountain@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-12 13:37:09 UTC-05:00
I would certainly be up for a trip to St. Louis for a gatheirng and I'm
certain Fox-Fyr would as well. Proably a few of the others from the Mid-MO
community as well. Of course finding a time when everyone's schedules jive
is the rough part.

I'm hosting the Mid-MO Fall Arts & Crafts Gatheirng on Nov 4 in Columbia if
you'd like to come down and meet the wonderful community we have. More
infor at C2B2's site www.midmoletterboxing.blogspot.com . Browse through
the archives too if you haven't before. Lots of good infor on how our scene
has developed.

As far as getting more boxes in your area, I think it's one of those things
that just can't be pushed. There's lots of publicity out about LB recently
(Games magazine just ran a good article) and so more folks are finding the
hobby, but as far as getting folks to plant, that is something they have to
do on their own. certainly encouraging them is worth the effort. I have a
few seceret series that I only give out clues to folks who have hidden 5 LBs
in the area. But I don't think this has encouraged more boxes because I
keep the whole thing tightly under wraps and spring it on them once they
fufill the criteria.

We (Mid-MO) just got lucky and have built a fantastic LB community, that
continues to grow by leaps and bounds. To me, that is part of the magic of
LB.

Back to a St Louis gatheirng, I think Forest Park would be a wonderful
place. I think there used to be (still?) a couple of LBs w/ great clues by
Dragonskald (?). Has anyone ever found those, or know if they are still
active? I'd be more than willing to help plan/organize a gathering with
you. The up-coming gatehirng in Columbia will be the fourth I've organized.

I should warn you, this won;t be the big, grand LB adventure I do in the
Spring. This is more focused on the arts and crafts that go into LB. Folks
have asked for a workshop gatheirng like this for some time, so I'm throwing
it out there. I'm expecting in excess of 30 people. We're doing workshops
on book binding, paper alteration, codes & ciphers and carving.

Whew! Take care!

Lnd-Crzr

From: Roze (rozebud@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-12 12:35:03 UTC-07:00
Hi Lnd-Crzr,

Thanks for the scoop on the 11/4 event - do I need to sign up, or just
show up? It sounds great - I really enjoy the arts & crafts aspect of
LBing, and would love to learn from some experts! Do you traditionally
host something at this time of year? I was kind of thinking fall 2007
for something in the Lou' (my job precludes anything in the spring, and
summer here is just miserable) and I wouldn't want to intrude on any
pre-existing events.

Dragonskald has changed his name to Tipsy Bard, and yes, his
"Ever-Fixed Mark" box is back in place - I rescued it for him this
spring, as the box had lost it's water-proof integrity and the log was
ruined. He planted a second box called "Sunny Softshell" but it didn't
last; it went missing after about the first month and he has yet to
replace it. I may do it for him...I've got the impression of the
original stamp, and would try to do a better camo job than he did
originally. (His clues are excellent and tricky poems, but he's the
first to admit that his carving needs work! We're planning to someday
collaborate, when life allows.)

I'm still quite the newbie - only started this past April - and I just
wish I lived somewhere with more boxes so I can just keep going and
going! I've been to Delaware (grew up there, don't ya know) twice
since I started, and there's SO MANY boxes in that area. Makes me
jealous!

I really appreciate both the invitation to your event and the offer of
assistance in planning one for St. Louis! Forest Park would be great
and very appropriate if I go with "Meet Me In St. Louie, Louie" for a
theme. I also have 8 boxes planted in Tower Grove Park, which is also
very lovely and has free pavilion rental (plus it's a short bike ride
from home).

Ok, Project Talk-The-BF-Into-a-Letterboxing-Weekend begins tonight!
I'll be in touch thru the blog. Thanks again!

rozebud
